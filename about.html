Our Story

The right way has always
been the harder way.

We started in 1999 with a simple idea: treat every customer's basement the way we'd want our own handled. No high-pressure sales. No surprise "site condition" charges after the work started. Just honest diagnoses, fair quotes, and work we'd be proud to put our name on.

A lot of contractors talk about integrity. We built a business on it — and it took 25 years to get the trophy to prove it. In 2025, the Better Business Bureau named us the Torch Award Winner for marketplace ethics. One winner. One year. One industry. That's us.

What does that actually mean for you? It means when we show up at your house, the person at your door has something to protect — a reputation that's worth more than any single job. That changes how the whole conversation goes.

What We Stand For

Three Rules. Non-Negotiable.

These aren't slogans printed on a wall. They're how every estimate, every job, and every follow-up call actually gets handled.

Rule One

If we wouldn't do it in our own home, we won't recommend it for yours.

Over-selling is the #1 complaint in this industry. We'd rather lose the sale than lose your trust. Our specialists are paid salary — not commission — so the right diagnosis is always the right answer.

Rule Two

The price we quote is the price you pay.

No mid-job change orders. No surprise fees for things we should have caught on the estimate. If something unexpected comes up, we stop, show you what we found, and get your permission before another dollar moves.

Rule Three

We come back. For life.

Our transferable lifetime warranty isn't fine print — it's the whole point. If the water comes back, we come back. Even if you sold the house. Even if it's 15 years later. That's the promise.

Ethics Matter.
The Award

It's not a star rating.
It's a verdict.

The BBB Torch Award doesn't go to the biggest company or the loudest ad budget. It goes to the business that, after a rigorous review of complaints, customer interviews, operational practices, and leadership interviews, has actually earned their reputation over years of consistent ethical conduct.

"Winners are organizations that exemplify the highest standards of ethics in marketplace practices, community relations, and internal business operations." — Better Business Bureau, Torch Award criteria

For a Wisconsin homeowner choosing a basement contractor, this is the single most meaningful credential to look for. It can't be bought. It can't be faked. It has to be lived.
